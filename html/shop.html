Coffee

It is one of the most consumed drinks in the world, omnipresent in our country. Without coffee, breakfast or after-dinner are not understood. But in addition to being one of the most consumed drinks in the world, the origin of coffee is a story full of curiosities.

The history of coffee begins in the horn of Africa, in Ethiopia, about the ninth century. The geographical origin is known, the province of Kaffa, but not the exact moment, since there are no documents on when man began to consume coffee beans.

Legend has it that a member of the Kaldi tribe, inhabitants of the higher areas of Ethiopia, observed how the goats had much more energy after eating a type of fruits, similar to cherries. By testing them himself, he discovered the energizing and exciting properties and transferred them to his tribe.

Whether or not this legend about the origin of coffee is true, what is certain and documented is that in the fifteenth century there were plantations in Yemen and a large coffee trade between Sudan and Arabia through the Yemeni port of Mocha. This city, in addition to giving its name to a type of coffee and a coffee maker, was also the main port on the route to Mecca. That is where the first establishments that served coffee appeared, the so-called "kaveh kanes". These premises soon became centers of social and political activity, even banned at various times.
